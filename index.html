Dr. Mauricio Cisternas

Data Scientist | Astrophysicist


I am an astronomer, currently working as a postdoc at the Instituto de Astrofisica de Canarias as part of Dr. Johan Knapen's group, and in collaboration with the S4G and DAGAL teams. I recently finished my PhD at the Max Planck Institute for Astronomy, working with Dr. Knud Jahnke as a member of his Emmy-Noether research group "Coevolution of Galaxies and Black Holes".

I am mainly interested in the relationship between galaxies and their central supermassive black holes. In particular, I focus on galaxies that host an active galactic nucleus, in other words, an accreting supermassive black hole. In this way, one can learn from the processes driving the periods of major black hole growth, and understand how the relation between galaxy and black hole evolves over cosmic time. If you are interested, you can read more about it on my research page.
